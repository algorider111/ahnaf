Competitive Programming — Game Plan

A clear, classic plan to level up your CF & competitive skills
Printable • Copy-friendly
1. Solve Problems 2. Skip Materials 3. Master DP 4. Focus Topics 5. Think 6. Learn 7. Never Miss a Contest

1. Solve Problems Around My Rating

Only solve problems that are close to your current rating level. Avoid problems that are either too easy or far beyond your reach — both are inefficient.

  • Target: 10 problems every day to build consistency and confidence.

2. Skip Irrelevant Materials

Stay laser-focused on the fundamentals. Don’t waste time on advanced structures that aren’t yet useful.

  • Example: skip persistent segment trees for now; return to them later when needed.
  • Keep practice practical — prefer problems that teach transferable techniques.

3. Master Dynamic Programming (DP)

DP is one of the most important skills. Train both recognition and technique.

  • Solve tag-free DP problems to learn recognition.
  • Solve tagged DP problems to practice specific techniques.
  • Target: 10 DP problems per day when focusing on this topic.

4. Focus Topics

Build strength in the core areas:

  • Number Theory
  • Greedy Algorithms
  • Mathematics
  • Implementation
  • Brute Force
  • Bitmasking
  • Graph Algorithms
  • Two Pointers
  • Binary Search

5. Think As Much As Needed

Spend real time reasoning before reading solutions — this is where growth happens.

  • Spend 30–40 minutes thinking about a problem before looking at solutions.
  • If you might solve it, read only the first line of the editorial for a hint and continue trying.
  • If completely stuck, read the full editorial and study others’ solutions closely.

6. Learn As You Solve

Don’t memorize ahead of time. Learn concepts on-demand while solving problems and immediately apply them.

  • Solve by tags; when a new technique appears, study it and reapply to the problem.

7. Never Miss a Contest

Contests are the fastest way to improve. Be consistent and analyze afterwards.

  • Participate in every Codeforces contest. If you can’t solve during the contest, upsolve immediately after.
  • Do 2 virtual contests per week for extra practice.
  • After each contest: solve missed problems and read others’ implementations for new tricks.
